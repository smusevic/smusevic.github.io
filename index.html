Our Care Commitment

Since we opened our doors in 2014, our aim at Zoo Education C.I.C (Zoo-Ed) has been to provide children with unique, quality animal inspired learning experiences.


At Zoo-Ed we cherish the special relationships we have with the animals in our care and believe that every child should have the opportunity to explore the human-animal bond. This unique bond is widely viewed as a support system which parallels the infant-parent relationship and can help us cope with emotional stress.


Animals are our world and as such we strive to provide our animals with the healthiest, safest and most nurturing environment that we possibly can. All our animals are kept at home and are very much part of the family.


Zoo-Ed are fiercely opposed to the cruelty of the international pet-trade and as part of our on-going commitment to animals, we ensure that the animals who come into our care are either sourced from responsible, local breeders or are rescued.


We believe that the mission of providing quality animal care should be a never-ending process of policy and review. Therefore, we constantly review our policies and procedures to provide the best possible care for our animals.


Our most recent policy under development was 'The Rule of Four'. This policy details that our animals will:


  1. Work for a maximum of 4 hours per day.
  2. Never experience travel time of more than 4 hours (2 hours each leg of the journey).
  3. Work a maximum of 4 days per week.
  4. Enjoy 4 consecutive days off each week.

Services

Animal Therapy

The therapeutic benefits of animal handling are well documented and we at Zoo-Ed are lucky enough to offer this service. We regularly attend nursing homes, supported learning centres, hospices and schools for the visually impaired and other special needs units. Depending on the number and needs of the individuals, we usually recommend a 1 to 1.5 hour session.

Educational Visits

All our school/nursery visits are underpinned by the Curriculum for Excellence (ACfE) and for younger children pre-birth to three. Our educational visits are carried out by Fiona McCrindle (BEd HONS) who is a fully qualified and registered teacher.
